LITERATURE 02.04
IN BETWEEN COLORS

Not everything in life is meant to be bold.

Not every moment is red - loud, passionate, undeniable.
Not every feeling is blue - deep, heavy, consuming.

Most of life exists somewhere in between.
In shades we don’t always have names for.

The Beauty of the Unnamed

We’re taught to label everything.
Happy.
Sad.
Success.
Failure.

But what about the feelings that don’t fit neatly into words?

The quiet contentment of sitting alone.
The strange emptiness after achieving something you once wanted.
The softness of a moment you know won’t last.


These are the in-between colors.
Subtle. Complex. Real.

A Life Not Meant to Be High Contrast

We often chase intensity.

Big wins.
Big emotions.
Big changes.

Because subtlety feels insignificant.

But think about art.
A painting made only of extremes, only black and white, can never capture the depth of something real.
It’s the gradients that make it alive.
The soft transitions.
The barely noticeable shifts.

That’s where the story lives.

You Are Not One Color

You are not just who you are on your best days.
Or your worst ones.

You are the unfinished sketch.
The smudged charcoal.
The watercolor that bled outside the lines.

You are layers.

Some visible.
Some hidden.
Some still forming.

Letting Things Be Undefined

There is a quiet freedom in not forcing clarity.

In not needing to explain every feeling.
In not rushing to define every phase of your life.

Sometimes, you don’t need to know exactly what something is.
You just need to experience it.

Like watching colors blend, without trying to separate them.

Maybe life was never meant to be understood in sharp lines.

Maybe it was always meant to be felt,
in gradients, in textures, in moments that don’t translate perfectly into words.

So let things be a little unclear.
A little unfinished.
A little undefined.


Because the most honest parts of life are the ones that exist
in between colors.🎨
