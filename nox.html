NOX

PROJECT SUMMARY
NOX is a high-energy mobile platform designed to bridge the gap between event discovery and the immersive experience of nightlife. While most ticketing apps focus on utility and data, NOX focuses on vibe and visual storytelling, catering to a demographic that values aesthetic and spontaneity.
PROBLEM STATEMENT
Mainstream event platforms often feel clinical, cluttered, and disconnected from the high-energy environments they represent. Users often find it difficult to "feel" the atmosphere of a party or club night through a standard list-style interface, leading to "decision fatigue."
MY ROLES
  • UI/UX Designer
  • Visual Branding & Logo Design
  • High-Fidelity Prototyping (Figma)
  • Information Architecture
  • Interaction Design
VISUAL IDENTITY
  • Color Palette: A high-contrast foundation of Onyx Black layered with Electric Pink and Deep Indigo gradients to mimic neon nightlife.
  • Typography: Bold, Modern Sans-Serif weights to create a "Brutalist" hierarchy that remains readable in low-light environments.
  • Design Language: A fusion of Sharp Geometric Edges and Atmospheric Glows, using minimal stroke-based icons for a premium, tech-forward feel.
  • Branding: A Custom Abstract Logo utilizing negative space and sharp symmetry to represent a "spark" or "star" of the night.
GOALS OF UI
  • Atmospheric Vibe: Dark mode with neon pink accents.
  • Quick Conversion: Sticky "Buy Now" button with high-contrast visibility.
  • Content Discovery: Poster-style grid layout to prioritize event artwork.
UI CHALLANGES & SOLUTIONS
  • Challange:
    • Balancing Vibrant Visuals with Legibility
    • Solution: I utilized dark card containers and semi-transparent overlays behind text to ensure high contrast and readability without sacrificing the brand’s energetic "vibe."
  • Challange:
    • Simplifying Event Discovery
    • Solution: I implemented a hierarchical grid layout. Large visual posters capture interest first, while secondary metadata is tucked into a focused, scannable footer within each card.
  • Challange:
    • Designing for Low-Light Contexts
    • Solution: I adopted an Onyx-first dark mode with a limited neon palette. This minimizes glare while ensuring the primary action—the "Buy Now" button—remains the most visible element on the screen.
